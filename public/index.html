Winter GameFest. 10 Amazing Years.

Winter GameFest is an annual electronic sports and video game culture event organized by the UCSD Sixth College Technology Committee. By facilitating face-to-face gaming rather than gaming in virtual environments, we help bring members of the video gaming community together in a celebration of gaming culture and tradition. Tournament settings provide high quality spectator experiences for UCSD students and unique opportunities to participate!

Logistics

Parking

The Gilman Parking Structure is the closest location for general parking. The Gilman Parking Structure is on the corner of Russell Lane and Gilman Drive, less than 5 minutes on foot from Price Center. Parking is free on Saturday and Sunday and $2 per hour on Friday in spots marked "visitor parking".

Participation: Free!

Both general attendance and tournament participation in Winter GameFest is free to UCSD students and the general public.

Registration

There is absolutely no entrance fee to both UCSD and non-UCSD associates, but all spectators and competitors must register to enter event grounds. Attendees not participating in tournaments can save time at check-in by registering ahead of time online, or register on the day of.

Tournament Participation

All tournament registrations will be done online, with the exception of Super Smash Brother: Melee, which will be walk-in. Each game has different requirements that will be announced when registrations are up.

Equipment

All Winter GameFest tournaments have a strict bring-your-own-controller or bring-your-own-computer policies. You are responsible for your own gear and potential damages caused by it. The Sixth College Technology Committee, UCSD University Centers, and UC San Diego are not responsible for any lost, stolen, or damaged equipment.

Code of Conduct

Winter GameFest attendees are expected to treat their fellow gamers courteously and respectfully. Fair play and good sportsmanship are core values of Winter GameFest. Misconduct may lead to expulsion from the event and tournaments as seen fit by event staff.

The first Winter GameFest was held in 2006 and had less than 50 attendees in a one-day format. Last year, Winter GameFest 2013 had 3,000 attendees including more than 500 competitors across 7 major title tournaments and ample opportunity for drop-in play. Winter GameFest has grown to be one of the largest student-run events on campus, and is a premier competitive video-game event in Southern California.
