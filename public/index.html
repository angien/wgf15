Winter GameFest.

Winter GameFest is an annual esports and video game culture event organized by the UCSD Sixth College Technology Committee. We host tournaments throughout the event accompanied by great music and a free play area for all of our attendees! By facilitating face-to-face gaming rather than gaming in virtual environments, we help bring members of the video gaming community together in a celebration of gaming culture and tradition.

Check out the WGF 2014 recap video!

Come join us! It's free!

Whether you come as a spectator or a competitor, there are a lot of opportunities to get some nice freebies and to learn more about video games at WGF 2015. We'll also have various arcade machines set up in our free play area!

Logistics. Important stuff.

Make sure to familiarize yourself with the following to get the most out of WGF.

Parking

The Gilman Parking Structure is the closest location for general parking. The Gilman Parking Structure is on the corner of Russell Lane and Gilman Drive, less than 5 minutes on foot from Price Center. All parking is free on Saturday and Sunday.

Participation: Free!

Both general attendance and tournament participation in Winter GameFest is free to UCSD students and the general public. While the event is open to people of all ages, tournament winners under 18 need a parent or guardian to sign additional paperwork.

Registration

There is absolutely no entrance fee to both UCSD and non-UCSD associates, but all spectators and competitors must register to enter event grounds. Attendees not participating in tournaments can save time at check-in by registering ahead of time online, or register on the day of.

Equipment

All Winter GameFest tournaments have a strict bring-your-own-controller or bring-your-own-computer policies. You are responsible for your own gear and potential damages caused by it. The Sixth College Technology Committee, UCSD University Centers, and UC San Diego are not responsible for any lost, stolen, or damaged equipment.

Code of Conduct

Winter GameFest attendees are expected to treat their fellow gamers courteously and respectfully. Fair play and good sportsmanship are core values of Winter GameFest. Misconduct may lead to expulsion from the event and tournaments as seen fit by event staff.

The first Winter GameFest was held in 2006 and had less than 50 attendees in a one-day format. Last year, Winter GameFest 2014 had 3,000 attendees including more than 500 competitors across 7 major title tournaments and ample opportunity for drop-in play. Winter GameFest has grown to be one of the largest student-run events on campus, and is a premier competitive video-game event in Southern California.
